Should Rubber Bands Be Used in Orthodontics?

A project exploring the mechanical behavior of orthodontic rubber bands under different loading and environmental conditions, with experimental testing and finite element analysis.

Introduction

Natural rubber is widely used in orthodontic elastics because of its elasticity and low cost, but its mechanical behavior can change under different loading conditions and environments.

Project Background

Orthodontic rubber bands are commonly used to apply force during treatment. Their effectiveness depends on how well they maintain force and resist deformation.

Why This Matters

Understanding how these elastics behave helps evaluate whether they remain reliable in clinical conditions such as repeated stretching, different temperatures, and pH exposure.

Aim of the Project

Research Goals

  • Determine how rubber bands deform under cyclic loading.
  • Assess whether environmental conditions affect their behavior.
  • Compare the force-displacement response of different band sizes.
  • Visualize deformation using finite element analysis (FEA).

Experimental Design

Materials

The study tested orthodontic rubber bands using controlled tensile and cyclic loading conditions. Measurements focused on displacement, strain, and force response.

Testing Conditions

  • Cyclic loading
  • Different temperatures
  • Different pH environments
  • Comparison of small and large diameter bands

Analysis Method

Experimental results were paired with finite element analysis to better understand how stress and deformation are distributed during expansion.

Results Overview

Key Findings

  • Rubber bands showed force loss after repeated loading cycles.
  • Temperature influenced mechanical response more strongly than pH.
  • Different band diameters produced different deformation behavior.
  • FEA helped visualize how strain develops as the band expands.

Conclusion

Final Takeaway

This project investigates whether rubber bands remain mechanically reliable for orthodontic use. By combining physical testing with finite element analysis, the study provides a clearer view of how elastic bands respond to repeated loading and changing environments.
